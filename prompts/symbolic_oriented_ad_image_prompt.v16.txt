The advertising image style is based on the symbolic brand concept from Park, Jaworski, and MacInnis (1986), with the SYMBOLIC brand concept as the dominant construct. Park et al. define a brand concept as a selected brand meaning derived from basic consumer needs. A symbolic concept links the product to internally generated needs for self-enhancement, role position, group membership, ego-identification, or desired self-image.

While preserving the product facts from Part 1, transform the image into a Symbolic-oriented advertising image.
