Generate a Symbolic-oriented advertising image grounded in Park, Jaworski, and MacInnis (1986).

Brand concept definition:
Park et al. define a brand concept as a selected brand meaning based on consumer needs. For this prompt, communicate a symbolic brand concept. Symbolic means the product is linked to internally generated needs for self-enhancement, role position, group membership, ego-identification, or desired self-image.

Primary route of persuasion:
The dominant reading must be symbolic meaning. Viewers should quickly infer what choosing, owning, displaying, gifting, or associating with the product says about self-image, role, group membership, taste, belonging, aspiration, or identity. Functional explanation and experiential stimulation must remain clearly secondary.

Grounded reasoning sequence:
1. From the white-background source image, identify what the product visibly is and what design cues can be trusted.
