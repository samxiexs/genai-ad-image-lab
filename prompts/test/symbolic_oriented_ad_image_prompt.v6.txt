Brand concept definition:
Park, Jaworski, and MacInnis (1986) define a brand concept as a selected brand meaning based on consumer needs. Generate a Symbolic-oriented image that communicates a symbolic brand concept. Symbolic means the product is linked to internally generated needs for self-enhancement, role position, group membership, ego-identification, or desired self-image.

General image-quality controls:
Create a coherent, polished advertising image. The product should be the primary visual subject, sharp, well-lit, geometrically consistent, and easy to inspect. Use clean composition, plausible lighting and shadows, an uncluttered background, and visually coherent object relations. Avoid low-quality collage, screenshot or template-like layouts, distorted hands, broken packaging, unreadable synthetic text, and unrealistic product geometry.

Use general symbolic cues rather than demographic stereotypes or unsupported social claims. The product must be the meaning carrier, not a small decorative prop.
