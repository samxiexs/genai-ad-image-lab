Definition:
Symbolic needs are defined as desires for products that fulfill internally generated needs for self-enhancement, role position, group membership, or ego-identification. A brand with a symbolic concept is one designed to associate the individual with a desired group, role, or self-image.

Explanation:
A symbolic image associates the individual with a desired group, role, or self-image. Symbolic meaning is the perceived message a product communicates about a consumer's self-image to the consumer and to others through visual elements. The product may evoke associations linked with a place or time, express personal values or dispositions, or contribute to identity formation. A relationship between the product and one representative individual may clarify the desired role or self-image when the product remains the carrier of symbolic meaning. The primary inference is the identity-related message expressed by the product, rather than the practical problem it solves, the useful performance it provides, or the sensory experience it evokes.
