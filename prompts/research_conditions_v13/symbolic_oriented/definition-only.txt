Definition:
Symbolic needs are defined as desires for products that fulfill internally generated needs for self-enhancement, role position, group membership, or ego-identification. A brand with a symbolic concept is one designed to associate the individual with a desired group, role, or self-image.

Explanation:
A symbolic image associates the product with a desired role, lifestyle, reference group, or self-image. Symbolic meaning should be perceived as the message the product communicates about the kind of person who chooses, owns, displays, carries, wears, or uses it. The image may express personal values, taste, expertise, status, achievement, or belonging through the product itself, recognizable brand cues, or contextual elements associated with a distinctive identity or way of life. A person may appear when doing so helps communicate the intended identity, but the product should be perceived as a natural part of that identity rather than as an isolated object being presented. The product should remain the primary source of symbolic meaning, while surrounding contextual cues reinforce the identity it symbolizes. The primary inference should be what choosing, owning, displaying, carrying, wearing, or using the product says about the associated person, rather than how the product solves a practical problem or what sensory stimulation it provides.
