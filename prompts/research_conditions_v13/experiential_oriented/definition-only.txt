Experiential-oriented Explanation:
An experiential-oriented image conveys the product's effect on sensory pleasure, variety, or cognitive stimulation. The image should highlight experiential aspects associated with encountering, using, or consuming the product by depicting a believable moment of product use or consumption. Rather than presenting the product as an isolated object, the image should communicate the lived experience of using it in an authentic, everyday context, allowing viewers to imagine or recall what the experience feels like from the user's perspective. Product-grounded cues, realistic use situations, and complementary elements may make the product's experiential qualities more vivid by suggesting how it is naturally handled, operated, enjoyed, or consumed. The primary inference should be what it feels like to encounter, use, or consume the product, rather than how the product solves a practical problem or what it communicates about the consumer's self-image, role, status, or belonging.
