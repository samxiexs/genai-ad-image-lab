Definition:
Symbolic needs are defined as desires for products that fulfill internally generated needs for self-enhancement, role position, group membership, or ego-identification. A brand with a symbolic concept is one designed to associate the individual with a desired group, role, or self-image.

Explanation:
A symbolic image associates the individual with a desired group, role, or self-image. Symbolic meaning should be perceived as the message the product communicates about self-image to the consumer and to others, based on visual elements of the product and its presentation. The product may evoke associations linked with a place or time, express personal values or dispositions, or help form identity. A single well-composed person may appear when that product-person relationship clarifies the desired self-image or role, while the product remains the main carrier of the symbolic meaning. The primary inference should be what choosing, owning, displaying, carrying, wearing, or using the product says about the associated person, rather than how the product solves a practical problem or what sensory stimulation it provides.
